Cultural Studies Research

Not the effects of media
but the habits of media practice

Emphasis on shared values: ideologies (what's
believed to be right) and worldviews (what's
believed to be true)

The linear transmission of messages matters
less than everyday rituals (Carey).

Culture is an activity and not something
we possess ("cultured").

Here's the "Culture" handout if you need it.

Common Questions

What are the habits of subcultures?
(the expressive rituals of subordinate groups)

What are the habits of perception?
(e.g., how we learn to look; the cis white male gaze)

What are the habits of performance?
(e.g., how others see us; impressions management
and the dramaturgy of communication)

Power is demonstrated publicly and
privately through media habits.

Discussion

Recall images and video:
How has framing taught you to look?

Recall the forms and models of comm:
How do content creators "break character"?

Or think about culture through specific works:
What's a comic, game, tv show, podcast, channel,
etc. that you care about, and how is it coded
for a particular community or subculture?
What habits does it address or encourage?
